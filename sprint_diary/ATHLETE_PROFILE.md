## 3. Injury History & Current Status

### Left Knee (MPFL/Patellofemoral)
**Diagnosis:** Chronic Medial Patellofemoral Ligament (MPFL) Overload / Micro-strain with Patellofemoral Maltracking

**Mechanism:**
- Excessive lateral pull (TFL/ITB/glute med fascial line) pulls patella outward
- Deep flexion forces MPFL to absorb extra tension causing micro-strain
- Pain reflexively inhibits VMO, destabilizing tracking (vicious cycle)

**Symptoms:**
- Unable to fully bend knee (affects heel recovery range)
- Unable to kick left heel to butt
- Pain after deep knee flexion under load (steep stairs, squats, deceleration)
- VMO feels "soft"/inhibited in extension, engages more at ~90 degrees flexion
- Pain eased by TFL/ITB release

**Physio Findings:**
- Kneecap hanging slightly higher than normal
- Some muscles connecting to kneecap are tight
- Hip is tight (adductors stretch well though)

**Management:**
- Deep squat holding (2-3 sets x 2-3 min, 2x/week) - leaves minor pain for 2 days
- Hamstring sliding for ROM
- Deep lunge with back straight, elbow to floor, front leg externally rotated (physio prescription)
- X-rays show no bony malalignment or arthritis (soft tissue issue)

## 7. Training Philosophy & Principles

### Core Methodologies
Following principles from: Charlie Francis, Glen Mills (Usain Bolt's coach), Ralph Oomen, Tony Holler (Feed the Cat/X-Factor)

### Key Principles

1. **Quality over Quantity**
   - Stop once quality drops (time increases 5-10%, form worsens)
   - Treat high-speed running like max-weight squats

2. **Consistency & Adaptation**
   - Keep sessions similar for 3-4 weeks so body can adapt
   - Don't try new things every week
   - Speed development needs 12+ weeks

3. **Periodization Flow**
   - General preparation → Specific preparation → Competition
   - Short-to-long with SE anchor (suits this athlete's profile)
   - All areas trained year-round, just different ratios/focus (Charlie Francis)

4. **High/Low Organization (Charlie Francis)**
   - Protect CNS, 48+ hours between high-intensity days
   - Never two high volume/intensity consecutive days
   - Day before acceleration/max speed: no running

5. **Progression Axes**
   - General → Specific (squat → quarter squat)
   - Volume → Intensity (5-6 reps 300m@60-80% → 2-3 reps 60-150m@98%)
   - Simple → Complex (A-skip → transition drills)
   - Strength → Speed (90% squat → 50% rapid lift)

6. **Session Focus**
   - Each session targets 1-2 specific goals only
   - Don't try to do everything in one session
   - Justify every part of the plan

### Weekly Structure (Current)
- **Monday:** Acceleration
- **Thursday:** Max Velocity
- **Saturday:** Speed Endurance
- **Gym:** Same day as sprint sessions (Jan-mid Feb), then can split to next day

### Cycle Structure
- 2+1 week plan: 2 training weeks + 1 deload week
- Pattern: Mid → High → Low OR High → High → Low
- Planning should be progressive: aim for 5% improvement per month

### Winter Gym Focus (Current Priority)
Given strong baseline strength (2.2x BW squat), focus on:
1. Eccentric force handling
2. Max effort overcoming isometrics
3. Plyometrics and elasticity
4. Prehab + rotational/core/movement training
5. Minimal hypertrophy (glutes only, some shoulder for aesthetics)
- NOT: slow concentric, heavy volume hypertrophy

## 9. Training Volume & Intensity Guidelines

### Sprint Training Buckets

| Type | Target | Volume/Session | Intensity | Rest |
|------|--------|----------------|-----------|------|
| **Acceleration** (10-40m, hills) | ATP-PC/alactic power, projection angles | 150-250m quality | ≥95% | 1-2 min per second of work (3-6 min for 30m) |
| **Max Velocity** (fly 10-30m) | Stiffness, vertical force, short contacts | 150-300m quality | ≥95% | 3-6+ min |
| **Speed Endurance** (80-150m) | Maintain velocity 7-15s, glycolytic | 300-600m | 92-97% | 6-10+ min |
| **Special Endurance I** (150-300m) | Anaerobic power/tolerance | 300-900m | 90-96% | 8-15 min |
| **Special Endurance II** (300-500m) | Lactate tolerance | 300-800m | 90-95% | 8-15 min |
| **Extensive Tempo** (100-200m) | Circulatory, recovery | 1200-2400m | 60-70% | 30-60s per 100m |
| **Intensive Tempo** (100-200m) | Bridge to SE | 800-1600m | 75-85% | 2-3 min per 200m |
| **Hills** (5-7% grade) | Acceleration, hamstring-friendly | Similar to accel | 90-95% | Full recovery |

### Wave Loading Pattern (3-Week)
| Week | Volume Level | Notes |
|------|--------------|-------|
| Week 1 (Low) | 80-90% of peak | Introduction/reload |
| Week 2 (Mid) | 90-100% of peak | Building |
| Week 3 (High) | 100% | Peak volume |
